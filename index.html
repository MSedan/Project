Habitat

In the UK, duck nests have been found in many places! Females will look for a new habitat, or place to live, that's covered in vegetation and plants, so that they can protect and hide their ducklings. Males will look for a place where there is lots of food.

Types of ducks

There are lots of different species of duck, but here are just a few:

  • Mallard - The most common duck found in the UK is the mallard duck. The males have green coloured heads with yellow bills, while the females have brown colouring all over. They are found all over the world, including the Americas, North Africa, South Africa, New Zealand and Australia.
  • Muscovy - The Muscovy duck has a striking red face with black and white feathers. They are native to Mexico and Central and South America.
  • Domestic - Most domestic ducks descend from the mallard duck, but some are descended from the Muscovy duck. They are raised as pets or for their eggs and down.
